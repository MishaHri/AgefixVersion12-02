Hyperhidrosis

Under Arms

Treatment

Hyperhidrosis treatment for excessive sweating typically involves the use of Botulinum toxin (commonly known as Botox). This treatment is effective for reducing excessive sweating in areas such as underarms, hands, and feet. The procedure involves injecting small amounts of Botulinum toxin into the affected areas, which works by blocking the nerve signals responsible for sweating. This treatment is safe, minimally invasive, and requires no downtime.

Pre-Treatment Advice:


  • Before the treatment, it's important to have a clean and shaved underarm area.
  • Avoid using any antiperspirant or deodorant 24 hours before the procedure.
  • Inform your practitioner about any medications you are taking or any allergies you have.

Post-Treatment Care:


  • Avoid strenuous exercise and exposure to heat (like saunas or hot showers) for at least 24 hours.
  • Refrain from massaging or applying pressure to the underarm area for a few days post-treatment.
  • Follow any specific aftercare instructions provided by your practitioner.

Results:

  • Significant reduction in sweating in the treated areas within 2 weeks.
  • The effects usually become noticeable within a week after treatment.
  • The results: typically last between 4 to 6 months, after which repeat treatments can be administered for ongoing sweat reduction.
  • Many patients experience improved confidence and comfort in social and professional situations due to the decrease in excessive sweating.

About the treatment:

What It Is
Hyperhidrosis treatment under the arms is a specialized procedure aimed at significantly reducing excessive sweating in the underarm area. This condition, known as axillary hyperhidrosis, can be socially and professionally inhibiting for those who suffer from it.

How It Works
The treatment typically involves the use of botulinum toxin injections (commonly known as Botox), which are administered directly into the underarm area. These injections work by blocking the nerve signals responsible for sweating, stopping the sweat glands from producing excessive sweat.

Is It Safe?
Yes, when performed by a qualified professional, this treatment is considered safe. The use of botulinum toxin for hyperhidrosis has been FDA-approved and is widely used for this purpose.
