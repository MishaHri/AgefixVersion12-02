Pre-Treatment:


  • Avoid alcohol, caffeine, and blood thinners like Ibuprofen, Ginkgo Biloba, and St John’s Wort 24 hours before your treatment.
  • Stay out of the sun and abstain from tanning treatments for one week leading up to the procedure ( use hats ).
  • Refrain from using products with acids for 48 hours prior to the treatment.
  • Make sure your skin is clean and free from any hair products on the day of your treatment

Post-Treatment:


  • Keep away from direct sunlight and ensure regular sunscreen application.
  • Stay well-hydrated and limit strenuous activities for a few days.
  • Refrain from using hair products or washing your hair for 24 hours following your treatment.
  • Avoid using saunas, steam rooms, and swimming pools for 48 hours post-treatment.
  • Postpone any intense physical exercise, as well as exposure to sun and heat, for at least 48 hours after your treatment

Results:

  • Stimulation of Hair Follicles: PRP contains growth factors that can rejuvenate the hair follicles. This leads to the stimulation of inactive or weakened follicles, promoting new hair growth.
  • Increased Hair Density: Many patients report a noticeable increase in the density of their hair. PRP can thicken existing hair, making the overall scalp look fuller.
  • Improved Hair Health and Texture: PRP treatments can enhance the health and texture of your hair. Hair often becomes stronger and more vibrant, which can improve its overall appearance.
  • Reduction in Hair Loss: PRP can effectively slow down the rate of hair loss, helping to preserve the existing hair for a longer period

  • Variable Outcomes: It's important to note that results can vary based on individual factors like age, genetics, the extent of hair loss, and overall health

Contraindications:

    The treatment it is not recommended for who presents one of the following condition:

  • Scalp infections or inflammatory skin conditions.
  • Alopecia areata (an autoimmune condition causing hair loss, where PRP may not be effective).
  • Blood clotting disorders.
  • Recent steroid injections to the scalp.

About the treatment:

What is PRP Therapy for the Scalp?

PRP Therapy for the scalp is an innovative treatment that uses your own blood's platelet-rich plasma to stimulate hair growth and improve scalp health. This procedure involves drawing a small amount of your blood, processing it to concentrate the platelets, and reinjecting it into your scalp.

How it Works: A small amount of blood is drawn from the patient and then spun in a centrifuge to separate the plasma and concentrate the platelets. This platelet-rich plasma is then injected back into the treatment area, releasing growth factors that stimulate natural healing processes, collagen production, and cell growth.

Is it Safe? Yes, PRP is generally safe as it uses the patient's own blood, minimizing the risk of allergic reactions or infections. However, like any procedure involving injections, there is a risk of bruising, swelling, or infection.

How Does PRP Therapy Promote Hair Growth?
The concentrated platelets in PRP are rich in growth factors that can stimulate dormant hair follicles, promote new hair growth, and improve the health and thickness of existing hair.

Who Benefits from PRP Scalp Therapy?
This treatment is ideal for those experiencing hair thinning, hair loss, or scalp health issues. It's suitable for both men and women seeking a natural approach to hair restoration.

Why Choose PRP for Scalp Therapy Over Other Hair Loss Treatments?
PRP Therapy is a natural, non-surgical option that uses your body’s own healing mechanisms. It's a great alternative for those who prefer a minimally invasive approach without relying on medications or surgical procedures.

What Results: Can I Expect?
Results: vary, but many clients experience a reduction in hair loss, regrowth of new hair, and an improvement in hair thickness and health. The full effects typically become evident a few months after treatment, as the hair growth cycle progresses.

How Many Treatments Are Required?
The treatment plan is tailored to individual needs. Generally, a series of 3-4 treatments spaced about a month apart is recommended, followed by maintenance sessions as needed.

Is There Downtime with PRP Therapy?
PRP Therapy for the scalp involves minimal downtime, allowing you to resume normal activities shortly after the treatment. Some clients may experience mild redness or tenderness at the injection sites.

Is PRP Scalp Therapy Safe?
Yes, PRP Therapy is a safe procedure that uses your own blood, minimizing the risk of allergic reactions or infections.
