Microneedling

Treatment

Microneedling, also known as collagen induction therapy, is a minimally invasive skin rejuvenation procedure. It involves the use of a device equipped with fine needles to create tiny punctures on the top layer of the skin. This process triggers the body's natural wound healing processes, resulting in cell turnover and increased collagen and elastin production. These effects can lead to firmer, smoother, and more toned skin.

Pre-Treatment Advice:


  • Avoid using topical products that contain tretinoin, retinol, retinoids, glycolic acid, or any "anti-aging" products (1-2 weeks before and after )
  • Refrain from consuming alcohol, coffee or using blood thinners such as Ibuprofen/Ginkgo Biloba/St Johnâ€™s Wort for 24hours prior to the treatment.
  • Avoid sun exposure and tanning treatments for a week prior.
  • Do not use retinoids or products containing acids for 48 hours before the procedure.
  • Ensure the skin is clean and free of any makeup or lotions on the day of treatment.


Post-Treatment Care


  • Use gentle, non-irritating skin care products.
  • Avoid direct sun exposure and apply sunscreen regularly.
  • Stay hydrated and avoid strenuous exercise for a couple of days.
  • Do not use makeup for at least 24 hours after the treatment.


Results

  • Visible improvements in skin texture and appearance can be seen after a few weeks.
  • For best results, a series of treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart is typically recommended.
  • The skin continues to improve over several months as collagen production increases.


Teatment Sumary

    What it is:
    Microneedling, also known as collagen induction therapy, is a cosmetic procedure that involves creating tiny punctures in the top layer of the skin using fine needles. This process triggers the body's natural wound healing processes, resulting in cell turnover and increased collagen and elastin production.
    How it Works:
    The fine needles used in microneedling create controlled micro-injuries to the skin, which in turn stimulates the body's natural wound healing mechanisms. This response promotes the production of collagen and elastin, essential proteins for maintaining skin's firmness and elasticity.
    Is it Safe?
    Microneedling is generally safe when done by a trained professional. It is suitable for most skin types. However, individuals with certain skin conditions or those who are pregnant should consult with a healthcare provider before undergoing the procedure.
