Pre-Treatment:


  • Avoid using topical products that contain tretinoin, retinol, retinoids, glycolic acid, or any "anti-aging" products.

  • Refrain from consuming alcohol or coffee 24 hours prior to the treatment.

  • Arrive for your appointment makeup-free, if possible, to facilitate a clean and efficient treatment process.

Post-Treatment:


  • Bruising and Swelling:
    Be prepared for potential bruising or swelling at the injection sites. This is a common reaction and usually subsides quickly
  • Stay Upright:
    Maintain an upright position and avoid bending over for at least 4 hours following the treatment.
  • Alcohol Consumption:
    Abstain from alcohol for 24 hours before and after your treatment to minimize bruising and swelling.
  • Avoid Heat:
    Steer clear of sun exposure, tanning beds, saunas, and hot tubs to ensure optimal healing.
  • Exercise:
    Hold off on intense physical workouts for up to 48 hours post-treatment.
  • Results:
    Timeline: Anticipate experiencing the full results: within up to 14 days after the treatment.
  • Communication:
    If you have any concerns or unusual reactions, reach out to the clinic for guidance.
  • Avoid using topical products that contain tretinoin, retinol, retinoids, glycolic acid, or any "anti-aging" products.
  • Arnica cream may be applied to bruises, and paracetamol is recommended for any discomfort.
  • Follow-Up:
    Consider scheduling a follow-up appointment in 2 weeks for a potential top-up, if needed.

Results:

  • You can expect to see results:
    within a few days, but it may take up to two weeks for the full effect.
  • The results:
    typically last between 3-5 months. Repeated treatments are necessary for maintaining the effects.
  • Some patients report minor bruising or swelling at the injection site, but these side effects are usually temporary.

Contraindications:

  • Allergy to botulinum toxin or any component of the formulation. Infection at the injection site.

  • Neuromuscular disorders (e.g., myasthenia gravis, Lambert-Eaton syndrome).

  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding.

  • Certain medications (like aminoglycoside antibiotics) that can interact with the treatment.


    • It's important to consult with a healthcare professional to assess individual risks and suitability before undergoing any of these treatments. Each individual's health status and medical history can influence the safety and effectiveness of these procedures.
