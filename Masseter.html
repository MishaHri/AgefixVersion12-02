Results:

  • Noticeable slimming and softening of the jawline for a more refined facial contour.
  • Reduction in the appearance of a square jaw, resulting in a softer, more oval or heart-shaped facial structure.
  • Relief from jaw tension and pain associated with teeth grinding.
  • The full effects are usually visible within 2-3 weeks post-treatment.
  • The slimming effect typically lasts for 4-6 months, after which follow-up treatments can maintain the desired appearance.

Contraindications:

  • Allergy to botulinum toxin or any component of the formulation. Infection at the injection site.
  • Neuromuscular disorders (e.g., myasthenia gravis, Lambert-Eaton syndrome).
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding.
  • Certain medications (like aminoglycoside antibiotics) that can interact with the treatment.


    • It's important to consult with a healthcare professional to assess individual risks and suitability before undergoing any of these treatments. Each individual's health status and medical history can influence the safety and effectiveness of these procedures.

Teatment Sumary:

Masseter reduction, also known as jawline slimming, is a non-surgical treatment aimed at reducing the size of the masseter muscles, which are located at the sides of the jaw. This procedure typically involves the injection of Botulinum toxin (Botox) into these muscles.

Botox helps in relaxing the muscles, thereby reducing their size and creating a more slender and aesthetically pleasing jawline.

This treatment is popular among those seeking a less square or bulky jaw appearance and can also help alleviate discomfort from teeth grinding (bruxism).
